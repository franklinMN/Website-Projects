ROMAN CATHOLICS

Celebrating faith and unity in the Catholic community.

TIMELINE

  • 1st Century: Formation of early Christian communities following the teachings of Jesus Christ.
  • 4th Century: The Roman Empire officially adopts Christianity under Emperor Constantine.
  • 1054: The Great Schism leads to the separation of the Western (Roman Catholic) and Eastern (Eastern Orthodox) branches of Christianity.
  • 1095-1291: The Crusades, a series of religious wars, are initiated by the Roman Catholic Church.
  • 1215: Fourth Lateran Council reinforces church doctrine and structure.
  • 16th Century: The Protestant Reformation challenges Roman Catholic teachings, leading to the formation of various Protestant denominations.
  • 1545-1563: The Council of Trent addresses issues raised by the Reformation and reaffirms Catholic doctrine.
  • 19th Century: Growth of Catholicism in response to industrialization and immigration.
  • 1870: The First Vatican Council defines the doctrine of papal infallibility.
  • 20th Century: Vatican II (1962-1965) introduces reforms to modernize and adapt the Church to contemporary issues.
  • 1980s-2000s: Pope John Paul II plays a significant role in global affairs and outreach.
  • 21st Century: Pope Francis becomes the first Pope from the Americas, emphasizing social justice and inclusivity.
  • 2016: Mother Teresa, known for her Catholic missionary work, is canonized as a saint.
  • 2018: The Catholic Church faces renewed scrutiny for cases of clergy abuse, prompting calls for reform.
  • 2020s: Ongoing efforts by the Church to address modern challenges, including social justice, environmental issues, and interfaith dialogue.
